# Asset Sourcing Bible

## Global visual direction
- Aim for grounded realism, not fantasy excess.
- The world should feel materially ancient and politically lived in, with technology hidden inside ritual surfaces.
- Favor sun-bleached stone, aged plaster, hammered bronze, charred timber, woven cloth, reed fiber, and packed earth.
- Keep overt science-fiction assets rare and only visible in hidden chambers, convergence spaces, and brief power moments.

## Purchase filter
- Buy modular kits first.
- Prefer packs with neutral materials that can be recolored and dirtied.
- Avoid hero props unless they are needed for:
  - the Radiant Voice chamber
  - the storm-ruler platform
  - the bronze lawgiver chamber
  - the convergence ship core
- Skip packs that are heavily fantasy-coded, Roman-imperial, or medieval.
- Prioritize packs that include collisions, LODs, and landscape-ready pieces.

## Egypt
### Must-have environment kits
- sandstone temple exterior set
- columned hall pieces
- necropolis corridor pieces
- floodplain village kit
- shoreline reeds and low agriculture

### Must-have props
- stelae
- offering tables
- sealed jars
- funerary cloth
- solar icon fragments
- granary storage props

### Visual rules
- oldest and most layered region
- surfaces should feel reused, repaired, and sacred
- reserve the cleanest gold and brightest light for hidden machine spaces only
- the archive depths should shift from ritual stone to impossible alignment without becoming neon sci-fi

### Greybox acceptable at first
- outer necropolis branches
- secondary village interiors
- minor market stalls

## Greece
### Must-have environment kits
- marble sanctuary kit
- mountain path kit
- cliffside retaining walls
- shrine court pieces
- small noble house courtyard pieces

### Must-have props
- bronze tripods
- votive statues
- rope winches
- shields and spear racks
- seating blocks
- storm altar pieces

### Visual rules
- emergent and performative region
- use brighter sky exposure and stronger silhouettes than Egypt
- stage views so the sanctuary feels like a theater for power
- the storm-ruler's spaces should mix aristocratic polish with improvised spectacle machinery

### Greybox acceptable at first
- noble house interiors
- lower village alleys
- secondary overlook spaces

## Italic west
### Must-have environment kits
- timber-stone hill settlement kit
- packed-earth road kit
- palisade and gate kit
- forge interior kit
- civic ring or ritual court pieces

### Must-have props
- boundary stones
- weights and measures
- anvils
- braziers
- road markers
- drafting tables
- legal tablets or carved markers

### Visual rules
- compact, practical, and formative
- geometry should feel younger and less monumental than Egypt
- law and infrastructure should be visible in roads, markers, and repeated measurements
- the bronze chamber is the one place where civic pragmatism becomes uncanny

### Greybox acceptable at first
- extra hill homes
- side-road branches
- non-essential forge outbuildings

## Convergence
### Must-have environment kits
- ruined stone shell pieces that can sit above the ship
- hidden chamber kit
- restrained sci-fi corridor/core kit
- cable, relay, and cryo-adjacent props

### Must-have props
- mission consoles
- sealed pods
- damaged bulkheads
- fractured display surfaces
- support pylons

### Visual rules
- keep the science-fiction reveal sparse and severe
- avoid glossy spaceship clichés
- lighting should contrast ancient dust with cold surviving machine light
- use this map to justify the hidden logic behind the prior three regions

### Greybox acceptable at first
- peripheral maintenance corridors
- secondary log rooms

## UI and typography direction
- avoid generic futuristic HUD styling
- use a restrained, carved, inscription-inspired serif for titles and a readable companion face for body text
- interface panels should feel like mapped notes, ritual diagrams, and salvaged field instruments rather than holograms
- use color by function:
  - pale gold for Light
  - storm blue-gray for Storm
  - muted green-white for Healing
  - iron umber for Craft
  - ash black for Death
  - ochre-white for Judgment
  - terracotta for Order
  - smoke violet-gray for Deception

## Reuse strategy
- one rock pack can serve all outdoor regions with material tuning
- one generic cloth/market pack can serve villages in Egypt and Greece
- one fire and dust VFX pack can serve all ancient spaces
- one high-quality metal material set can cover bronzes across Greece and the Italic west
- convergence should reuse as little as possible from the public ancient spaces
