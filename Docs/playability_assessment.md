# Many Names Playability Assessment

## Scope

This assessment reflects the current alpha after the integration, playability, and presentation pass. It is based on runtime/code inspection, authored-content review, world-build review, and the current generated asset stack.

## What Now Reads Correctly

- The five-region structure is coherent. Opening, Egypt, Greece, Italic West, and Convergence now present distinct ideological and visual identities instead of one blended "ancient world" read.
- The game now communicates a soft regional map for the three companion courts:
  - Egypt leans OracleAI through archives, funerary continuity, and interpretation.
  - Greece leans SkyRuler through spectacle, weather, and oath pressure.
  - Italic West leans BronzeLawgiver through roads, measure, and civic coercion.
  - Convergence is the collision point rather than a generic end map.
- Journal output is no longer just raw counters. It now surfaces region court, faction pressure, rumor state, domain resonance, unlocked powers, and quest-state structure.
- Dialogue scenes now carry belief-vs-truth framing and can trigger scene-linked temporary voice and audio profiles.
- Placeholder cinematic shells no longer imply total absence: the runtime now has a procedural cutscene fallback that can frame named actors through tagged camera anchors while proper sequence blocking catches up.

## What Still Reads Prototype-Grade

- Most cutscenes are still procedural fallback or lightly blocked placeholder sequences rather than hand-authored cinematic scenes.
- Audio is now more coherent and fully wired, but it is still obviously temporary/generated in timbre and mix.
- Named MetaHumans are in better shape than before, but wardrobe and movement variety are still below final-quality expectations.
- Egypt still reads as the most specific region. Greece, Italic West, and Convergence are substantially better integrated now, but they remain behind Egypt in landmark density and material storytelling.
- Crowd life is readable and present, but still staged rather than socially alive.

## Highest Current Player-Facing Risks

1. Cutscene quality mismatch
   - Runtime behavior is now safe and playable, but not yet emotionally impressive.
   - The fallback camera system prevents empty scenes, but some moments still feel like "camera on actor plus audio" instead of true cinematic blocking.

2. Audio credibility
   - The game is no longer effectively silent in key scenes.
   - The remaining problem is quality, not hookup: the generated voices and motifs still sound temporary.

3. Material repetition
   - The world build is structurally stronger than before, but repeated surface language can still flatten some spaces.
   - This is most visible in Greece and Italic West route connectors.

4. NPC behavioral thinness
   - Motion variation is better due to offset idle timing and profile tagging, but the crowd still reads as staged ambient presence rather than inhabited society.

## Recommended Next Pass

1. Hand-polish 8-10 hero cutscenes
   - Opening arrival
   - Opening witness aftermath
   - Egypt archive reveal
   - Greece storm omen
   - Italic forge-law confrontation
   - Convergence arrival
   - Three branch-distinct convergence/end scenes

2. Replace the weakest temporary voice tracks
   - Keep the same authored scene ids and audio profile ids.
   - Improve only the source material and mix.

3. Deepen landmark and material specificity in Greece, Italic West, and Convergence
   - More route storytelling
   - Stronger foreground props
   - Less repeated wall/floor language

4. Add one richer behavior tier for named and midground NPCs
   - Better stance sets
   - Look-at behavior in dialogue range
   - Limited ambient movement in hubs

## Readiness Summary

The project is now materially more playable than the prior prototype state. The main remaining gap is no longer systems breadth. It is presentation quality: cutscene blocking, audio quality, crowd vitality, and region-specific art density.
